# 📊 Sourcing Campaign Performance Dashboard

> Built with **Microsoft Excel + Power Query** | Hiring Period: Oct 2025 – Feb 2026

---

## 🗂️ Overview

This dashboard tracks end-to-end recruitment funnel performance across three lines of business: **Health & Wellness**, **Facility Services**, and **Realty Project**.

It was built to give the sourcing team and hiring managers a single source of truth — covering everything from applicant volume down to the actual hires, including drop-off points and candidate withdrawal reasons.

> ⚠️ **Note on Context:** This dashboard was produced during a period of significant team restructuring. Approximately half of the sourcing team was laid off mid-campaign, which directly impacted output — particularly for the Realty Project line of business. The metrics here reflect real performance under reduced capacity, making the overall results more meaningful, not less.

## 💡 Key Insights

### 1. 4% Conversion is Healthy Given the Context
For high-volume sourcing campaigns — especially with the Sourcing Team controlling 53% of lead generation — a 4% Source-to-Hire rate reflects strong top-of-funnel quality control, not poor performance.

### 2. Qualifications is the #1 Rejection Reason (484 cases)
This points to a recurring gap between applicant profiles and job requirements. Recommended fix: recalibrate sourcing strings or update JDs to better distinguish "must-have" vs. "nice-to-have" qualifications.

### 3. 71% of Rejections Happen at Sourcer Screening
This is actually a *win* — it means hiring managers are only spending time on the top ~29% of qualified talent. The team is gatekeeping effectively.

### 4. Non-Responsive is the #1 Withdrawal Reason (104 cases)
Classic "process lag" symptom. Candidates lose interest or get competing offers before we can move them forward. Fix: automated SMS follow-ups or tighter SLAs between Phone Screen and Manager Interview stages.

### 5. Realty Project Numbers Reflect Org Disruption, Not Poor Sourcing
With only 21 candidates processed vs. 1,031 (Health & Wellness) and 807 (Facility Services), the Realty Project gap is a direct result of the sourcing team for that vertical being let go mid-campaign — not a reflection of sourcing inefficiency.
